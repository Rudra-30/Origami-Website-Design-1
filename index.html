Some facts about Camels (Click the Image to find More!)

  • Camels are often called the Ship of the Desert.
  • Camels have adapted very well to the Deserts.
  • Camels have special eyelids which help them to stand against sandstorms.

Some facts about Teddy Bears (Click the Image to find More!)

  • Teddy bear is named after U.S.A's 20th President, Theodore or so called as "Teddy" Roosevelt.
  • The first Teddy Bear made was in November 14, 1902.
  • The largest Teddy Bear measures 19.41m (63ft 8in) in length, and was constructed by Municipio de Xonacatlán.

Some facts about Pandas (Click the Image to find More!)

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day.
  • Pandas like to lick copper and iron.
  • Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.

Some facts about Cicadas (Click the Image to find More!)

  • Cicada is a bug which has stout bodies, broad heads, clear membraned wings and large compound eyes
  • There are more than 3000 species of Cicadas.
  • In early Chinese folklore, cicadas were also considered high-status creatures that rulers should seek to emulate in their purity.
  • Female cicadas can lay up to 400 eggs at a single time.
